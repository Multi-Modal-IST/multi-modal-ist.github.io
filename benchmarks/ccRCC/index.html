MMIST ccRCC Benchmark

ccRCC is the most common type of kidney cancer, accounting for up to 80% of all renal cell carcinoma cases in adults. Estimating the prognosis is critical for patient management, but it is still a very challenging task. Ongoing research on this topic has led to the creation of two public studies: CPTAC-CCRCC and TCGA-KIRC, from which we curated MMIST-ccRCC.

MMIST-ccRCC Benchmark

MMIST-ccRCC offers a comprehensive benchmark for survival prediction in clear cell renal cell carcinoma (ccRCC) patients. It integrates multi-modal data (CT, MRI, WSIs, Genomics, and Clinical features) from CPTAC-CCRCC and TCGA-KIRC studies, simulating real-world variability and incomplete data scenarios. Recent works evaluate various patient-level prediction strategies, highlighting challenges and opportunities in multi-modal learning for oncology.

Benchmark Results

The following results summarize the performance of multiple methods evaluated on MMIST-ccRCC for survival prediction, based on two recent studies:

Method Strategy Modality Use C-Index Source
Majority Voting Aggregation Multi-modal 0.66 ± 0.04 Graph Aggregation (2024)
One-Dominance Aggregation Multi-modal 0.68 ± 0.03 Graph Aggregation (2024)
Multi-Instance Learning (MIL) Patient-level MIL Multi-modal 0.71 ± 0.02 Graph Aggregation (2024)
Single-Modal Baseline (WSI) Baseline WSI only 0.63 ± 0.03 MMIST-ccRCC (CVPRW 2024)
Single-Modal Baseline (Genomics) Baseline Genomics only 0.60 ± 0.04 MMIST-ccRCC (CVPRW 2024)
Multi-modal Fusion (Early Fusion) Fusion CT, MRI, WSI, Genomics 0.72 ± 0.02 MMIST-ccRCC (CVPRW 2024)

For detailed experimental setups and further insights, refer to the CVPRW 2024 MMIST-ccRCC paper and Graph-Based Aggregation Methods paper (2024).
